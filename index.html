Midnight Burn

It’s past midnight,
The fan hums like a tired thought.
I stare at the ceiling,
waiting for sleep to remember me.
The room isn’t dark,
but it feels blind.
Every corner holds something I’ve forgotten to feel.
And somewhere in between,
a name burns in silence.
Not pain,
just a dull echo of what used to matter.
No messages to reply to,
no voice that breaks the stillness.
Only me,
and the burn I don’t talk about.
